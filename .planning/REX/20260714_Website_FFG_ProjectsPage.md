# Projects Page Design Study

## Objective

Design a Projects page that makes the visitor want to enter a world rather than browse a catalogue.

---

## Foundations

Three ideas guided this work.

> Beauty emerges from engineering.

Beauty is the visible consequence of competent work.

> Industrial optimism.

Engineering, craftsmanship and science elevate humanity.

> Cultivated capability.

Mastery matters more than wealth.

---

## Observation

Although these references come from completely different industries, they express the same values.

They favour workmanship over decoration, proportion over spectacle and confidence over persuasion.

A perfectly crafted mahogany panel carries more dignity than gilded brass.

---

## Consequences

The website should present the work, not decorate it.

The project carries the emotion.

The interface frames it, then disappears.

---

## Interaction

Several directions were explored.

- Magazine covers
- Travel brochures
- Luxury catalogues
- Posters
- Physical plaques

The solution gradually converged towards a single full-screen image.

The visitor first discovers a destination.

They then approach it.

Finally, they enter it.

---

## Typography versus Plaque

Two directions remained.

### Typography

The project title appears directly on the artwork.

Simple.

Timeless.

The image remains the centre of attention.

### Enamel Plaque

A navy enamel plaque with a brass inlay creates a distinctive identity, but also risks recalling the skeuomorphic interfaces of the early 2000s.

---

## Decision

Version 1 adopts typography.

It is quieter, easier to execute and lets the artwork carry the identity.

The plaque remains an interesting future direction if it solves a real problem rather than satisfying an aesthetic temptation.

---

## Principle

> Mastery does not need ornament.
